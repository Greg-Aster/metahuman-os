# Fresh Pasta Dough Practice Guide

## Practice Recipe: Traditional Italian Egg Pasta
*Serves 4-6*

### Ingredients
- **300g flour blend**:
  - 165g Tipo 00 flour (55%)
  - 135g fine semolina flour (45%)
- **3 large eggs** (room temperature)
- **1 egg yolk**
- **Extra semolina** for dusting

### Equipment Needed
- Large wooden board or clean counter
- Fork
- Bench scraper
- Plastic wrap
- Clean kitchen towel

## Step-by-Step Practice Instructions

### 1. Setup (5 minutes)
- Bring eggs to room temperature (30 minutes before starting)
- Measure flours precisely using kitchen scale
- Clear a large workspace (at least 24" x 24")
- Have plastic wrap ready

### 2. Create the Well (2 minutes)
- Mix 00 flour and semolina together
- Pour onto work surface
- Use your hands to form a volcano shape
- Create a well in center about 6 inches wide
- Walls should be about 1 inch thick

### 3. Add Eggs (3 minutes)
- Crack eggs directly into the well
- Add the extra yolk
- Using a fork, beat eggs gently in the center
- Gradually pull in flour from inner walls
- Keep outer walls intact to prevent spillage

### 4. Initial Mixing (5 minutes)
- Continue incorporating flour with fork
- When mixture becomes too thick for fork, switch to hands
- Gather all flour into rough dough
- Don't worry if it looks shaggy at this point

### 5. Kneading Phase (15-20 minutes)
**The Traditional Italian Method:**

a) **Push Phase**: Using heel of your palm, push dough away from you
b) **Fold Phase**: Fold dough back over itself
c) **Turn Phase**: Rotate dough 45 degrees
d) **Repeat**: Continue this rhythm for 15-20 minutes

**What to Feel For:**
- First 5 minutes: Dough will feel rough and uneven
- 5-10 minutes: Starting to smooth out, becoming elastic
- 10-15 minutes: Smooth, supple, slight tackiness
- 15-20 minutes: Silky smooth, springs back when poked

### 6. The Tests (2 minutes)

**Poke Test**: Press finger into dough - should spring back slowly

**Cut Test**: Cut dough in half - look for tiny, evenly distributed air bubbles

**Window Test**: Stretch a small piece - should stretch thin without tearing

### 7. First Rest (30 minutes)
- Form dough into smooth ball
- Wrap tightly in plastic wrap
- Let rest at room temperature
- Dough will hydrate and relax during this time

### 8. Final Check
After resting, unwrap and check texture:
- Should feel noticeably softer
- More pliable and stretchy
- Ready for rolling and shaping

## Troubleshooting Your Practice

### If Dough is Too Dry:
- Spray lightly with water
- Knead to incorporate
- Let rest 10 minutes
- Reassess

### If Dough is Too Wet:
- Dust with small amount of flour
- Knead briefly
- Avoid adding too much flour

### If Dough Tears When Stretched:
- Needs more kneading
- Or needs more rest time
- Check hydration level

## Success Indicators

✓ **Color**: Bright, uniform yellow throughout
✓ **Texture**: Silky smooth with subtle tackiness
✓ **Elasticity**: Springs back when poked
✓ **Structure**: Tiny air bubbles visible when cut
✓ **Workability**: Rolls out without tearing after rest

## Next Steps

Once you've mastered the dough:
1. Practice rolling techniques (by hand or machine)
2. Learn different pasta shapes
3. Perfect cooking times (2-3 minutes for fresh)
4. Pair with your marinara sauce

Remember: Great pasta dough takes practice. Each batch teaches you something new about feel, timing, and technique. The goal is to develop an intuitive understanding of the dough's needs.
