FRESH PASTA & MARINARA SAUCE - GROCERY LIST
==========================================

FRESH PASTA DOUGH (serves 4-6)
------------------------------
□ 2 cups (240g) "00" flour (Tipo 00) - Italian fine-milled flour preferred
  Alternative: All-purpose flour (but "00" gives silkier texture)
□ 1/2 cup (60g) Semolina flour (for dusting and texture)
□ 4 large eggs (room temperature)
□ 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
□ 1 teaspoon fine sea salt

CLASSIC MARINARA SAUCE
----------------------
□ 2 cans (28 oz each) whole San Marzano tomatoes
  Alternative: High-quality whole plum tomatoes
□ 6 cloves fresh garlic
□ 1 large yellow onion (optional - for smoother sauce)
□ 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil (good quality)
□ 1 bunch fresh basil leaves
□ 1 teaspoon dried oregano (or 1 tablespoon fresh)
□ 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)
□ 1 tablespoon sugar (to balance acidity)
□ Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

FINISHING TOUCHES
-----------------
□ 1 block (8 oz) Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese (for grating fresh)
□ 1/4 cup fresh Italian parsley
□ Extra virgin olive oil (for drizzling)
□ Coarse sea salt (for pasta water)

OPTIONAL EQUIPMENT TO CONSIDER
------------------------------
□ Pasta machine or roller (if not using rolling pin)
□ Pasta drying rack
□ Large stock pot (for cooking pasta)
□ Wooden cutting board (for kneading)

SHOPPING TIPS:
-------------
• "00" flour can be found at Italian markets or specialty stores
• San Marzano tomatoes are worth the extra cost for authentic flavor
• Buy eggs from local farms if possible - fresher eggs make better pasta
• Get a good chunk of real Parmigiano-Reggiano, not pre-grated
• Fresh herbs make a significant difference in the marinara

QUANTITIES NOTE:
This list provides enough ingredients to make pasta and sauce for 4-6 people, 
with extra sauce that can be stored in the refrigerator for up to a week.
