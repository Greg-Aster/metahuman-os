# Pacific Crest Trail Thru-Hike Itinerary
## Southern Terminus to Northern Terminus (2,654 miles)

### Hiker Profile Considerations
- Age: 43 years old
- Health: Mild heart disease (requires conservative pacing and regular rest days)
- Starting fitness: Building up from unemployed/sedentary lifestyle
- Motivation: Life experience, personal growth, escape from stagnation

### Recommended Approach: Conservative Pace
- **Target daily mileage**: 12-18 miles (building up gradually)
- **Total duration**: 5-6 months
- **Start date**: Late April (avoid early season heat in desert)
- **Rest days**: Every 5-7 days minimum (heart health priority)

---

## PHASE 1: SOUTHERN CALIFORNIA DESERT (Miles 0-702)
### Weeks 1-6: Building Trail Legs

#### Week 1: Campo to Mount Laguna (Mile 0-42)
- **Day 1**: Campo (Mile 0) → Hauser Creek (Mile 15.4) - 15.4 miles
- **Day 2**: Hauser Creek → Lake Morena (Mile 20) - 4.6 miles (SHORT DAY - recovery)
- **Day 3**: REST DAY at Lake Morena (resupply, adjust gear)
- **Day 4**: Lake Morena → Boulder Oaks Camp (Mile 26.1) - 6.1 miles
- **Day 5**: Boulder Oaks → Cibbets Flat Camp (Mile 32.6) - 6.5 miles
- **Day 6**: Cibbets Flat → Mount Laguna (Mile 42) - 9.4 miles
- **Day 7**: REST DAY at Mount Laguna (elevation adjustment)
**Week total**: 42 miles in 7 days (6 miles/day average)

#### Week 2: Mount Laguna to Scissors Crossing (Mile 42-77)
- Building to 10-12 miles/day with regular breaks
- **Day 8-14**: 35 miles over 7 days
- Key stops: Pioneer Mail, Sunrise Trailhead
- Focus on heat management and hydration

#### Week 3: Scissors Crossing to Warner Springs (Mile 77-109.5)
- **Days 15-21**: 32.5 miles over 7 days
- First major resupply at Warner Springs
- REST DAY at Warner Springs Community Center

#### Week 4: Warner Springs to Idyllwild (Mile 109.5-179)
- **Days 22-28**: 70 miles (increasing to 12-15 miles/day)
- Elevation gain practice for Sierra
- Side trip to Idyllwild for resupply

#### Week 5: Idyllwild to Big Bear (Mile 179-266)
- **Days 29-35**: 87 miles
- Fuller Ridge, San Jacinto alternates
- REST DAY in Big Bear City

#### Week 6: Big Bear to Wrightwood (Mile 266-369)
- **Days 36-42**: 103 miles
- Deep Creek Hot Springs
- Cajon Pass (McDonald's!)

### Weeks 7-10: Central California Desert

#### Week 7: Wrightwood to Tehachapi (Mile 369-566)
- **Days 43-49**: 197 miles split over 2 weeks
- Agua Dulce, Green Valley
- Building to consistent 15-18 miles/day

#### Week 8-9: Tehachapi to Kennedy Meadows (Mile 566-702)
- **Days 50-63**: 136 miles
- Final desert push
- Extra rest before Sierra entry

#### Week 10: Kennedy Meadows Preparation
- **Days 64-70**: REST AND PREPARATION
- Gear swap (bear canister, microspikes)
- Weather assessment
- Physical recovery before Sierra

## HEALTH & SAFETY PROTOCOLS

### Daily Health Checks:
- Morning heart rate monitoring
- Hydration assessment (urine color)
- Foot care routine
- Joint/muscle evaluation

### Heart-Healthy Hiking:
- Start slow each morning (15-minute warm-up)
- Take 10-minute breaks every 2 hours
- Monitor exertion level (conversational pace)
- Extra rest days when needed

### Emergency Protocols:
- Carry InReach or similar emergency beacon
- Share location with trusted contact daily
- Know nearest exit points for each section
- Carry comprehensive first aid kit

## PRE-HIKE TRAINING PLAN (3 Months)

### Month 1: Base Building
- Walk 3-5 miles daily
- Add weight gradually (start with 10 lbs)
- Focus on consistency over speed

### Month 2: Elevation Training
- Increase to 8-10 mile hikes weekly
- Find hills/stairs for elevation
- Practice with full pack weight (25-30 lbs)

### Month 3: Peak Training
- Back-to-back hiking days
- 15-20 mile days on weekends
- Test all gear systems

## FINAL THOUGHTS

Remember: This journey is about personal growth and healing. Listen to your body, especially given your heart condition. There's no shame in taking extra rest days or adjusting plans. The goal is to complete the trail safely while having a transformative experience.

"Hike your own hike" - Don't compare yourself to younger or faster hikers. Your journey at 43 with health considerations is just as valid and impressive as anyone else's.

The PCT will challenge you physically and mentally, but it also offers incredible rewards: stunning landscapes, trail community, and time for deep reflection away from Portland's stagnation. This could be exactly the reset you need.

Safe travels and happy trails! 🥾
