# Epic Summer Adventure Plan 2025
## PCT Southern Terminus to Portland → 2026 Solar Eclipse in Europe

### 🏔️ PCT Hiking Plan (Southern Terminus to Portland, OR)

#### **Permits & Passes**
- **PCT Long-Distance Permit**: FREE, but limited to 50 hikers/day March-May
  - Required for 500+ mile continuous trips
  - Must start on designated date and location (very strict!)
  - Apply at permit.pcta.org
  - Only one permit per person per year
- **California Fire Permit**: Required for thru-hiking
- **Peak Season**: March 1 - May 31 (limited permits)
- **Start Date Recommendation**: Mid-to-late April 2025

#### **Route Distance**
- Southern Terminus (Campo, CA) to Portland, OR: ~1,100-1,200 miles
- Estimated hiking time: 3-4 months at 15-20 miles/day average

#### **Optimal Timing**
- **Start**: Mid-to-late April 2025
- **Finish in Portland**: July-August 2025
- **Weather Benefits**:
  - Avoid extreme desert heat in SoCal
  - Hit Sierra Nevada during optimal snow conditions (June-July)
  - Reach Oregon during prime hiking season (July-August)
  - Complete before fall weather/early snow

#### **Key Weather Considerations**
- **Southern California (April-May)**: Desert conditions, moderate temps
- **Sierra Nevada (June-July)**: Snow may still be present at high elevations
- **Northern California/Oregon (July-August)**: Prime hiking weather
- **Water**: Heavy loads in SoCal (20+ mile carries), abundant in Oregon

### 🎒 Essential Ultralight Gear List

#### **"Big Three" (Target: <3 lbs total)**
- **Backpack**: Gossamer Gear Mariposa 60 (~2 lbs) or ULA Circuit (68L)
- **Shelter**: ZPacks Duplex (1 lb 3 oz) or Hyperlite Mountain Gear Mid 1P
- **Sleep**: Western Mountaineering UltraLite 20°F bag + Therm-a-Rest NeoAir XLite pad

#### **Target Base Weight**: 10-15 lbs (ultralight threshold)

#### **Critical Desert Gear**
- **Water capacity**: 6-8 liters for long desert carries
- **Sun protection**: Hat, sunglasses, long sleeves, sunscreen
- **Navigation**: GPS device + paper maps (cell service unreliable)

#### **Money-Saving Tips**
- Resupply at towns vs. mailing boxes (more flexible)
- Camp free vs. paying for lodging when possible
- Cook own meals vs. restaurant meals
- Consider used/borrowed gear vs. buying new

### ✈️ 2026 Solar Eclipse in Europe

#### **Eclipse Details**
- **Date**: August 12, 2026
- **Duration**: Up to 2 minutes 18 seconds of totality
- **Time**: Late afternoon/sunset

#### **Best Viewing Locations**

**SPAIN (Recommended - Best Weather)**
- **A Coruña, Northwest Spain**: Top choice for clear skies
- **Bilbao & Santander**: Direct path of totality
- **Zaragoza**: Urban viewing option in totality path
- **Weather**: Hot, dry August = 70-80% clear sky probability
- **Bonus**: Can combine with Camino de Santiago pilgrimage

**ICELAND (Alternative - Dramatic Landscapes)**
- **Western Iceland**: 2+ minutes of totality
- **Weather**: Only 30-40% clear sky chance in August
- **Temperature**: 40s-60s°F
- **Bonus**: Unique Arctic landscapes

#### **Travel Planning Tips**
- **Book accommodations NOW**: Hotels book up months/years in advance
- **Arrive 2 days early**: Weather backup and location scouting
- **Flight from Portland**: PDX → Madrid/Bilbao (via connections)
- **Budget consideration**: Spain generally more affordable than Iceland

### 🏥 Health & Safety Considerations

#### **Physical Preparation**
- Start training 3-4 months before (cardio + hiking with pack)
- Break in all gear thoroughly
- Address any existing health issues before departure

#### **On-Trail Safety**
- **Communication**: Satellite communicator (Garmin InReach, SPOT)
- **Emergency contacts**: File trail plan with trusted contacts
- **Medical kit**: Comprehensive first aid, personal medications
- **Water treatment**: Multiple methods (filter + purification tablets)
- **Weather monitoring**: Be prepared to exit in severe conditions

#### **Money-Saving Benefits of Wilderness Living**
- **No rent/utilities** for 3-4 months
- **Minimal food costs** with smart resupply strategy
- **No entertainment/dining expenses**
- **Estimated savings**: $3,000-5,000 vs. city living
