# Portland, Oregon Housing Discrimination Cases and Resources

## Overview
This document provides comprehensive information about housing discrimination cases, laws, and resources in Portland, Oregon, compiled from various legal sources and recent settlements.

## Legal Framework

### Federal Law
- **Title VIII of the Civil Rights Act of 1968** (Federal Fair Housing Act)
- **42 United States Code Section 3604**

### Oregon State Law
- **ORS 659A.421** - Oregon's general rules against housing discrimination
- **OAR 839-003-0200** - Administrative rules for filing housing discrimination complaints

## Protected Classes

Fair Housing laws prohibit discrimination based on:

### Federal Protected Classes:
- Race
- Color
- National origin
- Religion
- Sex
- Familial status (families with children under 18)
- Disability (physical or mental)

### Additional Oregon Protected Classes:
- Sexual orientation
- Source of income
- Marital status
- Immigration status
- Survivors of domestic violence, sexual assault, stalking, or bias crimes

## Recent Major Cases and Settlements (2023-2024)

### 1. Randall Group Settlement (March 2024)
- **Settlement Amount**: $850,000
- **Parties**: Inclusive Communities and Fair Housing Council of Oregon vs. Randall Group
- **Issue**: Discriminatory tenant screening practices with disparate impact on Black, Latinx, Native American renters, and people with disabilities
- **Filed**: December 2022

### 2. Portland Property Management Companies Settlement (July 2024)
- **Parties**: Community Alliance of Tenants and Housing Justice Project (represented by ACLU of Oregon) vs. multiple Portland-area property management companies
- **Issue**: Discriminatory tenant screening practices, particularly regarding criminal history screening
- **Outcome**: Monetary relief for tenants and required changes to screening practices

### 3. Holland Partner Group/Holland Residential Settlement (December 2023)
- **Settlement Amount**: $1.9 million
- **Agency**: U.S. Department of Justice
- **Issue**: Discrimination against families with children through occupancy policies
- **Violation**: Fair Housing Act

## How to File a Complaint

### 1. Oregon Bureau of Labor and Industries (BOLI)
- Investigates housing discrimination complaints
- Must file within **1 year** of the discriminatory act
- Can file in person or by mail at any BOLI office in Oregon

### 2. U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD)
- Federal agency that investigates housing discrimination
- Must file within **1 year** of the discriminatory act

### 3. Fair Housing Council of Oregon (FHCO)
- Provides assistance with:
  - Understanding your rights
  - Investigating complaints
  - Gathering evidence
  - Educating landlords
  - Referrals to lawyers or government agencies

### 4. Private Lawsuit
- Can file in court within **2 years** of the discriminatory act
- May want to consult with an attorney

## Complaint Requirements

A formal complaint must include:
- Written statement signed by the aggrieved person
- Names and addresses of all parties involved
- Description of the discriminatory acts
- Explanation of how the person was harmed
- Date(s) of the discrimination

## Documentation Tips

To prepare for a potential case, keep records of:
- All documents, letters, and contracts
- Receipts and financial records
- Emails and text messages
- Voicemail messages
- Written notices
- Photos or videos (if relevant)
- Names and contact information of witnesses
- Timeline of events

## Key Takeaways from Recent Cases

1. **Screening Criteria**: Even "neutral" screening criteria can be discriminatory if they have a disparate impact on protected classes

2. **Criminal History**: Blanket policies regarding criminal history can violate fair housing laws

3. **Family Status**: Occupancy policies that limit families with children are illegal

4. **Enforcement is Active**: Multiple major settlements in 2023-2024 show continued enforcement of fair housing laws
