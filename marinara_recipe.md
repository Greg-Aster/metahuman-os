# Classic Italian Marinara Sauce Recipe

## Ingredients (Serves 4-6)
- 2 (28-ounce) cans whole San Marzano tomatoes (DOP-certified preferred)
- ½ cup extra virgin olive oil (high quality)
- 4-6 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 8-10 fresh basil leaves
- Sea salt to taste

## Equipment Needed
- Large saucepan or wide skillet (12-14 inches preferred)
- Wooden spoon
- Can opener
- Bowl for crushing tomatoes

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Preparation (5 minutes)
1. Open the cans of tomatoes and pour them into a large bowl with their juice
2. Using clean hands, crush the tomatoes into chunks (about 1½ inches)
   - Don't over-crush - you want some texture
   - Keep the juice - it's part of the sauce
3. Peel and slice garlic cloves thinly (about ⅛ inch thick)
4. Have basil leaves ready but keep them whole

### Cooking Process (20 minutes)

#### Step 1: Heat the Oil (2 minutes)
- Place saucepan over medium-low heat
- Add ½ cup olive oil
- Heat until oil shimmers but doesn't smoke
- Test readiness: a small piece of garlic should sizzle gently when added

#### Step 2: Cook the Garlic (2 minutes)
- Add all sliced garlic to the oil
- Stir gently with wooden spoon
- Cook until garlic turns pale golden (NOT brown)
- If garlic cooks too quickly, add 1 tablespoon water to slow it down
- The aroma should be sweet and nutty, not sharp

#### Step 3: Add Tomatoes (1 minute)
- When garlic is perfectly golden, immediately add crushed tomatoes with all juice
- The mixture will bubble and splatter - this is normal
- Stir to combine oil and tomatoes
- Add ½ teaspoon salt initially

#### Step 4: Simmer the Sauce (15 minutes)
- Bring to a gentle simmer (small bubbles, not a rolling boil)
- Partially cover the pan, leaving space for steam to escape
- Simmer for exactly 15 minutes, stirring every 5 minutes
- After 10 minutes, taste and adjust salt as needed

#### Step 5: Finish with Basil (Last 5 minutes)
- In the last 5 minutes of cooking, tear basil leaves and add to sauce
- Stir gently to distribute
- Remove from heat immediately after the 15-minute mark

### Quality Checks

Your marinara is perfect when:
- Color is bright red, not dark or brownish
- Consistency coats a spoon but isn't too thick
- Taste is bright and tomatoey with garlic sweetness
- No raw tomato flavor remains
- Basil aroma is fresh and present

### Serving Suggestions

1. **With Fresh Pasta**: Toss with your homemade pasta and a splash of pasta water
2. **Let it Rest**: For best flavor, let sauce rest 10-30 minutes before serving
3. **Storage**: Refrigerate up to 5 days or freeze up to 3 months

### Common Variations (Not Traditional but Popular)

- **Arrabbiata**: Add red pepper flakes with the garlic
- **Puttanesca**: Add olives, capers, and anchovies
- **Amatriciana**: Add guanciale or pancetta

### Troubleshooting

- **Too acidic**: Your tomatoes may not be San Marzano. Add a tiny pinch of sugar
- **Too thick**: Add pasta water or regular water to thin
- **Too thin**: Simmer uncovered for 5 more minutes maximum
- **Bitter taste**: Garlic was overcooked or basil added too early

### The Secret to Success

Remember: Less is more. The beauty of marinara is its simplicity. Trust the process, use quality ingredients, and don't overcook. This sauce celebrates the pure flavor of tomatoes enhanced by garlic, olive oil, and basil - nothing more, nothing less.
