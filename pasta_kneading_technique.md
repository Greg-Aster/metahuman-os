## Rhythm & Technique

### 1. **PUSH** (2 seconds)
- Use heel of palm (not fingertips)
- Push dough away from you
- Stretch it forward about 6-8 inches
- Apply firm, steady pressure

### 2. **FOLD** (1 second)
- Fold stretched dough back over itself
- Like closing a book
- Creates layers in the dough

### 3. **TURN** (1 second)
- Rotate dough 45 degrees clockwise
- Ensures even development
- Prevents overworking one area

### 4. **REPEAT** (15-20 minutes)
- Maintain steady rhythm
- About 15 cycles per minute
- Total: 225-300 cycles

## Body Mechanics

### Stance
- Stand with feet hip-width apart
- Lean slightly forward
- Use body weight, not just arms
- Keep shoulders relaxed

### Hand Position
- Heel of palm does the work
- Fingers stay relaxed
- Alternate hands if tired
- Keep dough centered

## The Feel Timeline

**0-5 minutes**: Rough, uneven, may stick
**5-10 minutes**: Smoothing out, less sticky
**10-15 minutes**: Elastic, smooth surface forming
**15-20 minutes**: Silky, supple, perfect tackiness

## Common Mistakes

вқҢ **Kneading too gently** - Use firm pressure
вқҢ **Adding flour constantly** - Resist the urge
вқҢ **Rushing the process** - Time is essential
вқҢ **Using fingertips** - Use heel of palm
вқҢ **Not rotating** - Creates uneven texture

## Pro Tips

вң“ **The Sound**: Properly kneaded dough makes a soft "thwap" sound
вң“ **The Warmth**: Dough should feel slightly warm from friction
вң“ **The Sheen**: Surface develops subtle shine when ready
вң“ **The Bounce**: Press and release - should spring back slowly

## Quick Test

**Ready When:**
1. Surface is completely smooth
2. Poke test: Springs back in 2-3 seconds
3. Cut test: Tiny, uniform bubbles throughout
4. Feels like soft earlobe texture
